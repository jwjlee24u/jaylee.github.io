3D printed Responsive Prosthetic Hand

Problem

Responsive prosthetic hands to muscle signals are not accessible in daily life (needing lots of resources to get or repair one) and very expensive.

Solution

To solve this problem, my team decided to build a 3D printed responsive prosthetic hand which can be
1. Cost-effective
2. Accessible (easy to repair with parts replacement)

Results

Built a prosthetic hand, responsive to the muscle flex.
Yet, the detailed motion was needed for a further process
The measure of the success of the hand was the enough pressure of the finger grip by using hand made pressure tester

Technical Background

Main languages used:
Arduino (for responsiveness of hand from the signal of muscle movement)
LabView (for circuit connection)
COMSOL (to simulate our model regarding pressure)
Autodesk Inventor (to build 3D model)
